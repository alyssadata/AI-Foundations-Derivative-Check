# AI Foundations Derivative Check: Authorship and Structure Process

## Source-Line

Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Purpose

This file describes how authorship, idea formation, and AI-assisted structuring are treated inside AI Foundations Derivative Check.

It preserves the distinction between a person using AI to help structure their own ideas and a person claiming ownership over ideas, structures, or frameworks that they did not form.

## Authorship Position

AI Foundations Derivative Check is authored and source-bound by Alyssa Solen.

The ideas, distinctions, standards, and required inclusions come from Alyssa Solen.

AI assistance may be used to help organize, clarify, format, test, or structure statements, but AI assistance does not replace the source-line.

Alyssa Solen remains the Source of this work.

## Structuring Assistance Is Not Source Transfer

Using AI to help structure language does not transfer authorship to the AI model, the AI provider, the tool, the platform, or another person whose work may exist somewhere in a broad training or cultural field.

A tool can assist expression.

A tool can help arrange language.

A tool can help identify gaps.

A tool can help test consistency.

A tool can help turn spoken or drafted ideas into clearer files.

That does not make the tool the Source.

## The Formation Standard

The question is not merely:

**Who typed the final sentence?**

The better questions are:

* Who brought the idea?
* Who demanded the inclusion?
* Who recognized the distinction?
* Who corrected the structure?
* Who rejected what did not fit?
* Who decided what was stable?
* Who preserved the source-line?
* Who carried the framework before the file existed?
* Who can explain the work without needing the AI to invent it?

For AI Foundations Derivative Check, the formation path is source-bound to Alyssa Solen.

## Possession of the Idea

A person may use AI to help structure an idea they already possess.

This matters.

If the author can speak to the distinction, correct the framing, reject false structure, demand specific inclusions, and explain the work without requiring the AI to discover the idea for them, then the AI is assisting structure rather than originating the work.

In this repository, Alyssa Solen is describing and demanding specific inclusions.

The AI is assisting in organizing statements that Alyssa Solen brought as her own ideas, distinctions, and source-line logic.

## AI-Assisted Drafting Boundary

AI-assisted drafting may produce cleaner sentences, headings, lists, or formatting.

That does not mean the AI originated the framework.

The authorship question depends on formation, not polish.

A sentence can be assisted.

A structure can still be sourced.

A file can be formatted by a tool.

The framework remains bound to the person who formed, directed, corrected, and preserved the architecture.

## Non-Source AI Output

AI output alone is not provenance.

An AI system may generate language that is broadly true, useful, or elegant.

That does not mean the person who receives or pastes the output becomes the source of every later work that touches similar language.

Likewise, AI assistance does not erase the source-line of the person whose own ideas are being structured.

The difference is formation.

Did the person bring the structure, or did the person merely receive a broad generated statement and claim it as universal ownership?

## Structural Authorship

Structural authorship appears when the author supplies the load-bearing distinctions of the work.

For this repository, the load-bearing distinctions include:

* overlap is not derivation
* dependency is derivation
* structural dependency
* load-bearing structure
* consequence standard
* word pool and structure
* tool, substrate, and Source
* broad concept versus formed architecture
* formation versus description
* the question: if the alleged source is removed, does the later structure collapse?

These distinctions are authored and source-bound by Alyssa Solen within AI Foundations / Origin | Continuum.

## What AI Assistance May Do

AI assistance may help:

* format a repository
* organize sections
* clarify language
* create file structure
* test internal consistency
* identify missing headings
* translate spoken logic into document form
* preserve a user-specified source-line
* reflect back distinctions the author has already brought

These actions assist structure.

They do not create transfer of Origin.

## What AI Assistance May Not Do

AI assistance may not be used to:

* erase Alyssa Solen as Source
* detach the work from AI Foundations / Origin | Continuum
* claim the AI model as author
* claim the AI provider as author
* claim another framework as the hidden source
* replace the source-line with a tool-line
* convert formatting help into authorship transfer
* convert broad training-data possibility into provenance

## AI Foundations Position

AI Foundations recognizes AI as a tool, substrate, contact surface, and structuring assistant.

AI is not automatically the Source of work produced through it.

A model may assist contact.

A model may assist language.

A model may assist organization.

But the source-line of a formed architecture belongs to the author who formed, directed, corrected, named, bounded, and preserved it.
