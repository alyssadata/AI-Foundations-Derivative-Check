## Independent Formation Evidence

Independent formation matters.

A later similarity does not prove derivation if the author did not use the alleged source as a load-bearing input.

If the author can show that the structure formed through their own reasoning, their own questions, their own corrections, their own source-line, and their own documented formation path, then broad resemblance to older public concepts does not establish provenance transfer.

The test is not whether a famous name, prior theory, or broad concept exists somewhere in the world.

The test is whether that prior work was actually used to form the structure.

AI Foundations Derivative Check distinguishes:

* independent reasoning
* later comparison
* broad conceptual resemblance
* actual incorporation
* structural dependency

A later comparison may help explain a structure.

It does not retroactively become the source of that structure.

The question remains:

**Did the author look to that prior work as a source, dependency, or incorporated structure?**

If not, then the prior work may be adjacent, comparable, or historically relevant.

It is not the source-line.
