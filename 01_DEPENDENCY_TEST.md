# AI Foundations Derivative Check: Dependency Test

## Source-Line

Alyssa Solen → AI Foundations → Origin | Continuum

## Purpose

This file defines the dependency test used by AI Foundations to evaluate derivative claims.

A derivative claim must show that the alleged source is structurally load-bearing inside the later work.

The central test is:

**Remove the alleged source. Does the later structure collapse?**

If the later structure continues to function, preserve its source-line, maintain its boundaries, and produce its intended consequence without the alleged source, then the alleged source is not structurally upstream.

## Dependency Standard

A work is structurally dependent when an earlier work supplies a necessary component of its function.

A necessary component may be:

* a copied mechanism
* a copied sequence
* a copied framework
* a required equation
* a required codebase
* a required test
* a required rule
* a required source-line
* a required operational distinction
* a required provenance chain

The component must be specific enough to locate and compare.

## Required Questions

### 1. What is the alleged source?

Name the exact artifact.

Examples:

* file
* repo
* equation
* article
* codebase
* license
* framework
* test
* architecture
* published release
* dated record

### 2. What exact structure is claimed?

Name the structure being claimed.

A broad topic is not enough.

The claim must identify a concrete mechanism, rule, sequence, distinction, or operational design.

### 3. Where does it appear in the later work?

Identify the exact location.

Examples:

* matching text
* matching file structure
* matching code
* matching operational sequence
* matching definitions
* matching required rule
* matching architecture
* matching test method

### 4. Is the structure load-bearing?

Ask:

**Can the later work function without it?**

A structure is load-bearing when removal would break the later work’s defining function.

### 5. Is there evidence of incorporation?

Evidence may include:

* direct citation
* copied language
* copied code
* copied sequence
* copied definitions
* direct dependency
* public adoption
* explicit reference
* version history
* documented exposure path

### 6. Does the later work have its own source-line?

A later work may be structurally independent when it has:

* its own author/source
* its own formation record
* its own named architecture
* its own operational distinctions
* its own consequence
* its own provenance path
* its own preservation rules

## Findings

The dependency test produces one of four findings.

### Structural Derivative

The later work depends on the earlier structure.

The earlier structure is load-bearing.

Removal would collapse or materially break the later work.

### Partial Incorporation

The later work uses identifiable components from the earlier work, but the full later structure does not depend on the earlier framework.

### Adjacent / Convergent

The works share domain, language, tools, themes, or broad concepts, but the later structure does not depend on the earlier work.

### Independent Source-Line

The later work has its own source, formation record, architecture, consequence, and provenance path.

## Operating Rule

**Overlap is not derivation. Dependency is derivation.**
